Cable Structure

The structure of cables is frequently the overlooked element in the equation of the perfect IT formula. Often a poor structure, possibly built up over time in an ad hoc fashion, is the main reason why companies experience bad connections and a slow performance.

A cable structure is a complicated thing. There are many different types of cable and they are all intended for different tasks and at different capacities. Imagine a road network that has London connected to Manchester with a single A road, but has sleepy Tonbridge connected to nearby Tunbridge Wells with a looping seventy-mile six-lane motorway. We have seen the equivalent of this in the cable infrastructure of many companies.
